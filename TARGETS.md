## CASL: how to email cold and stay legal
Canadian anti-spam law allows business outreach under **implied consent** when:
1. You email an address the person/business has **conspicuously published** (e.g. on their site) **without** a "no unsolicited email" notice, AND
2. Your message is **relevant to their role/business**, AND
3. You **identify yourself** (real name, business, mailing address), AND
4. You include a working **unsubscribe**.

Keep volume low and personalized. That's legal *and* it actually converts.

## The honest truth about an "autonomous marketing agent"
A bot that **auto-sends cold messages and auto-replies at scale is not compliant** — the autonomy-at-scale-without-consent *is* the CASL violation, and it blacklists your domain. So there are only two legitimate kinds of automation:

1. **Inbound / consent funnel (the real "autonomous, compliant" marketing):**
   Your **free tools are the magnet** — `reo detect` (free ransomware scan), `clean`, `shrink`. Offer a "free ransomware check" → visitor **opts in** with their email (that's consent) → *then* automated nurture is 100% legal because they asked for it. This scales without spamming. **Build this.**

2. **Founder-led outreach with AI draft-assist (human-in-the-loop):**
   You pick a company → REO/AI drafts a personalized, CASL-compliant email → **you** review and send → when they reply, AI drafts a suggested response → **you** send. Compliant (you're the sender), and high-converting because it's personal.
